If your goal is **natural conversation and professional communication**, phrasal verbs are even more important than idioms. Native speakers use them constantly.

Here are the **100 most common and useful phrasal verbs** to learn.

# ⭐ Essential Daily Phrasal Verbs

| Phrasal Verb | Meaning              | Example                  |
| ------------ | -------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Wake up      | Stop sleeping        | I wake up at 6 AM.       |
| Get up       | Leave bed            | I get up early.          |
| Turn on      | Switch on            | Turn on the lights.      |
| Turn off     | Switch off           | Turn off the TV.         |
| Sit down     | Take a seat          | Please sit down.         |
| Stand up     | Rise to your feet    | Everyone stood up.       |
| Go out       | Leave home/socialize | We went out for dinner.  |
| Come in      | Enter                | Come in, please.         |
| Go back      | Return               | I need to go back home.  |
| Come back    | Return               | I'll come back tomorrow. |

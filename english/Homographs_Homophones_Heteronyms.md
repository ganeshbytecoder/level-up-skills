If your goal is to become highly fluent in English, learning **homographs** (often mistakenly called "homograms") is extremely useful because they improve **reading comprehension, pronunciation, vocabulary, and speaking**.

### Types you should know

1. **Homographs** – Same spelling, different meanings (sometimes different pronunciation)

   * *lead* (to guide) vs *lead* (the metal)
   * *tear* (rip) vs *tear* (from your eye)

2. **Homophones** – Same pronunciation, different spelling/meaning

   * *right*, *write*, *rite*

3. **Heteronyms** – A special type of homograph where pronunciation changes

   * *wind* (moving air) vs *wind* (to twist)

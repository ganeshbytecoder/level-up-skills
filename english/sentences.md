This is one of the highest-ROI areas for improving communication.

Most English learners know grammar, but they struggle because they don't know **what native speakers actually say in different situations**. Functional English gives you ready-made sentence patterns for real conversations.

If you master these patterns, you'll become much more confident in meetings, interviews, presentations, casual conversations, emails, and negotiations.

Below is a comprehensive course organized by function.

---

* Good morning.
* Good afternoon.
* Good evening.
* It's nice to meet you.
* Pleasure to meet you.
* How have you been?
* It's been a while.

> Good morning, everyone. It's great to see you all today.

# 31. Professional Workplace English (Especially Useful for Tech)

These are very common in software engineering, meetings, and project discussions:

### Updating Progress

* I'm currently working on...
* I've completed...
* I'm waiting for...
* The task is on track.
* There's a blocker.
* I'm almost done.
* We should finish by Friday.

### Discussing Problems

* We've identified the root cause.
* The issue appears to be...
* We're investigating it.
* This needs further analysis.
* We can reproduce the issue consistently.
* A temporary workaround is available.

### Giving Recommendations

* I recommend...
* We should prioritize...
* It would be more efficient to...
* A better approach would be...
* I suggest we revisit this later.

### Asking for Input

* What are your thoughts?
* Do you have any concerns?
* Does anyone have suggestions?
* Am I missing anything?
* I'd appreciate your feedback.

# Daily Goal

Aim to learn and actively use:

* **10 new functional phrases per day**
* **3 short dialogues** using those phrases
* **1 real-life scenario** (meeting, shopping, interview, casual conversation, etc.)

In about **2–3 months**, you'll have internalized several hundred practical expressions that make your English sound much more natural.

## My recommendation for your learning plan

Based on our previous conversations, your goal is to communicate confidently in **meetings, technical discussions, presentations, and everyday conversations**. I'd prioritize these topics in this order:

1. Functional English (this course)
2. Conversation fillers and discourse markers
3. Storytelling frameworks (STAR, PREP, Situation–Problem–Solution–Result)
4. High-frequency collocations
5. Advanced connectors and transition phrases
6. Workplace and leadership communication

These skills will complement the pronunciation, phrasal verbs, idioms, and vocabulary you've already been studying, helping you speak more naturally and effectively in professional settings.

What you've listed are mostly **English fixed expressions, linking phrases, discourse markers, and common chunks**. Learning these is one of the fastest ways to sound natural because native speakers use them constantly.

There are **hundreds**, but if you master the most common 300–500, your speaking and writing will improve dramatically.

## What to learn next?

After idioms and phrasal verbs, the next high-impact area is:

1. Fixed expressions / chunks (this list)
2. Collocations (make a decision, take a break, heavy rain, strong coffee)
3. Sentence patterns (There is..., It seems that..., I would rather...)
4. Conversation fillers (Actually, Basically, Well, You know, I mean...)
5. Discourse markers (However, Therefore, Moreover, Nevertheless...)
6. Functional English (agreeing, disagreeing, persuading, negotiating, storytelling)

If you master:

* 300 phrasal verbs
* 300 idioms
* 500 collocations
* 300 fixed expressions/chunks

you'll know about **1,400+ high-frequency English patterns**, which cover a huge percentage of everyday conversations, meetings, presentations, and professional communication.
